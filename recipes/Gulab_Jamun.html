Gulab Jamun

this is indian dish you can see above and its name gulab jamun.

Gulab Jamun is a soft, deep-fried Indian dessert soaked in fragrant sugar syrup. Made with milk powder, these golden-brown dumplings are flavored with cardamom and saffron, making them perfect for festive occasions.

Juicy and rich, Gulab Jamun absorbs the syrup, creating a melt-in-the-mouth texture. Serve them warm or chilled, garnished with nuts, or pair with ice cream for an extra indulgence.

Ingredients

  1. For Gulab Jamun Balls:
    • 1 cup milk powder
    • ¼ cup all-purpose flour (maida)
    • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
    • ¼ cup milk (warm)
    • 1 tsp lemon juice or yogurt
    • Ghee or oil for frying
  2. For Sugar Syrup:
    • 1 ½ cups sugar
    • 1 ½ cups water
    • 2-3 cardamom pods (crushed)
    • 1 tsp rose water (optional)
    • A few strands of saffron (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup:
    • In a saucepan, mix sugar and water. Stir and bring to a boil.
    • Add cardamom pods and saffron strands. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes until slightly sticky.
    • Turn off the heat and add rose water if using. Keep it warm.
  2. Make the Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
    • Add ghee and mix with fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Gradually add warm milk and lemon juice/yogurt, mixing gently to form a soft dough.
    • Cover and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  3. Shape the Dough Balls:
    • Divide the dough into small portions and roll into smooth, crack-free balls.
    • Keep them covered to prevent drying.
  4. Fry the Gulab Jamun:
    • Heat ghee or oil over low-medium heat. The oil should be warm, not too hot.
    • Gently add the balls and fry, stirring continuously, until they turn golden brown.
    • Remove and drain on a paper towel.
  5. Soak in Sugar Syrup:
    • Immediately transfer the fried jamuns into the warm sugar syrup.
    • Let them soak for at least 2-3 hours for a soft, juicy texture.
  6. Serve and Enjoy:
    • Serve warm or chilled, garnished with chopped pistachios or almonds.
    • Enjoy as is or pair with vanilla ice cream for an extra treat!
